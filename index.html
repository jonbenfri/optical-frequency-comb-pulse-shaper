Interpretation notes

The solid curve is the physical intensity envelope |E(t)|². The dashed curve is the real part of the synthesized field in a line-index reference frame; it is useful for seeing square, triangle, and sawtooth Fourier-series behavior.

With only eight lines, discontinuous targets cannot be exact. Square and sawtooth targets show finite-bandwidth rounding and ringing, while triangle-like targets are usually cleaner because their Fourier coefficients decay faster.
